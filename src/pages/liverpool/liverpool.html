23 Liverpool - Maritime Mercantile City

The importance and the effect of a possible future UNESCO label’s removal on the citizens’ lives

Projektbeschreibung

Liverpool: An endangered city?

Liverpool is one of the most known cities in the Western Europe and in the U.K. While going through the UNESCO list looking for my research topic, I found out with great wonder that its nominated site, Liverpool – Maritime Mercantile City, nominated just in 2004, has been removed in 2009 into the „In Danger City‟, next to sites that are mainly found in war zones. Why and how did a famous western European city end up there?, was what triggered my curiosity to look more into this inscription. Developmental plans, which have been proposed but not yet implemented and which threaten the skyline and the authenticity of the waterfront was the answer.

My research

My interest turned then to the people living and working in the city of Liverpool. I wanted to find out their opinion about the subject (nomination and further inscription in the „In Danger List‟), their already made experience (e.g. demolition of historical buildings) and their fears and hopes for whatever the future may hold. That is why I went to Liverpool to talk with people – through semi-structured interviews – and learn, among other topics, what role the UNESCO label plays in their lives and if a future label retrieval would have an impact on their city.

My results

All interviewees were aware of the existence of the UNESCO label and all named either the waterfront as a whole or specific buildings or a specific area at the waterfront as the nominated area. To all was clear though that it was not the city as a whole that carries the label, but a part of it. The reasons for the nomination can be, according to the interviewees, purely historical, referring mostly to the emigration to America and less to slavery, architecture, maritime innovations and achievements.
